Draft Questions

How does the draft work?

We use a snake draft format where the order reverses each round (1-5, 5-1, 1-5, etc.). The draft order is randomly determined just before drafting begins. Each participant has 60 seconds to make their pick.

Once a college team is drafted, it's removed from the available pool and cannot be selected by another player.

Why are there conference restrictions?

The conference restrictions (only one team per conference per player) are designed to:

  • Encourage diversity in team selection
  • Prevent one player from loading up on all the top teams from one conference
  • Make the draft more strategic and research-intensive
  • Give mid-major programs more relevance in our league

These restrictions are lifted when conference play begins on January 1st.

Roster Questions

How many teams can I have on my roster?

The target roster size is 15-25 college teams per player, but you can go slightly under or over this range based on trades and waiver moves.

There's no strict penalty for being outside this range, but rosters significantly larger than 25 may be subject to league review to maintain competitive balance.

When do conference restrictions lift for waivers?

Conference restrictions are lifted when conference play begins on January 1st. After this date:

  • You can pick up additional teams from conferences where you already have one team
  • There are no limits on the number of teams you can have from a single conference
  • All other waiver rules still apply

This change reflects that conference play creates more differentiation between teams from the same conference.

Scoring Questions

How are upsets calculated?

Upsets are defined as:

  • An unranked team beating a ranked team (AP or Coaches Poll)
  • A lower seed beating a higher seed in March Madness

For upset victories, you earn double the regular points for that game. For example, a regular season upset win would be worth 2 points instead of 1, and a March Madness Round of 64 upset would be worth 4 points instead of 2.

Do First Four games count differently?

First Four games are treated like regular March Madness games with these specifics:

  • Worth 2 points per win (same as Round of 64)
  • Don't receive the round multiplier that later rounds get
  • Can qualify as upsets if they meet the criteria (lower seed beating higher seed)
  • Count toward your team's total March Madness wins

Transaction Questions

How do trades work?

Our trade process:

  1. Any player can propose a trade to another player
  2. Both parties must agree to the trade terms
  3. The trade is announced to the league
  4. League members have 24 hours to vote
  5. Trades require majority approval to pass (minimum 3 votes in a 5-player league)
  6. Commissioner processes approved trades

Trade deadline is March 1st at midnight ET.

What's the waiver wire process?

Waivers work as follows:

  • Dropped teams go on waivers for 24 hours
  • Claims are processed daily at 12pm ET
  • Priority goes to last-place team first (reverse standings order)
  • After processing, unclaimed teams become free agents
  • Free agents can be added immediately without waivers

Note: You can't claim a team if it would violate conference restrictions (before Jan 1st).
